Earthquake Alert System

The project focuses on designing an earthquake monitoring and warning system to detect earthquakes and alert people to take precautions. It also stores data for professionals in the field. The system uses real-time Twitter messages and a burst detection algorithm to quickly identify seismic events. Detected events are shared via a dedicated Twitter account and email notifications. The system successfully detects earthquakes of around 3.5 magnitude, with minimal false positives, often providing alerts faster than official channels. This information benefits government agencies and news outlets in reducing the impact of earthquakes and sharing timely updates.

The microcontroller's efficiency ensures continuous monitoring with minimal power consumption, making it an indispensable tool for healthcare professionals and personal wellness tracking alike.

Academic Projects (Part II)

Design of a Solar powered grid-tie inverter

The design of a Solar powered grid-tie inverter involves the integration of dual-stage buck and boost converters within the system, facilitating efficient power conversion and management. Leveraging PSIM software, engineers can simulate and optimize the performance of the inverter, ensuring seamless integration with the grid while maximizing solar energy utilization. This sophisticated design not only enables grid-tie functionality but also enhances overall system reliability and renewable energy utilization.

Design of Micro-strip Patch Antenna at 2.4

The design of a Micro-strip Patch Antenna at 2.4 GHz for wireless communication involves utilizing CST Studio Suite software for comprehensive electromagnetic simulation and analysis. Engineers meticulously optimize antenna dimensions and substrate materials to achieve desired performance parameters such as gain, bandwidth, and radiation pattern. By leveraging CST Studio Suite's advanced capabilities, precise tuning of the antenna structure ensures efficient transmission and reception in the 2.4 GHz frequency band, crucial for various wireless communication applications.

Design of an Obstacle Detector Circuit

The design of an Obstacle Detector Circuit using LM358 Op-Amps entails configuring the op-amps in a comparator mode to detect changes in infrared (IR) sensor readings. By integrating IR sensors with LM358 op-amps, the circuit distinguishes between ambient and obstructed environments, triggering appropriate output signals. This design enables the detection of obstacles within a predefined range, facilitating applications such as automated navigation systems or proximity sensors.

Design of a Smart Home Automated Temperature Control System

The design of a Smart Home Automated Temperature Control System involves utilizing PIC Compiler for programming and Proteus for simulation. Integrated with temperature sensors and actuators, the system dynamically monitors indoor temperature levels and adjusts heating or cooling devices accordingly. Through PIC Compiler, engineers develop firmware to process sensor data and execute control algorithms, while Proteus enables virtual testing and validation of the system's functionality before deployment. This seamless integration of hardware and software components ensures efficient temperature regulation, enhancing comfort and energy efficiency in smart homes.

IOT Home Automation System with Security System

The design of an IoT Home Automation System with a Security System using a microcontroller involves integrating various sensors, actuators, and communication modules to enable remote monitoring and control of home appliances and security devices. The microcontroller serves as the brain of the system, interfacing with sensors such as motion detectors, door/window sensors, and cameras to detect intrusions or unusual activities.

Thesis Topic

Design & Development of an energy efficient solar powered portable oil extractor.

The project focuses on the design and development of an energy-efficient solar-powered portable oil extractor. This innovative device aims to provide a sustainable solution for extracting oil from various seeds using renewable energy sources, specifically solar power. The project addresses the growing demand for efficient and environmentally friendly oil extraction methods, particularly in remote or off-grid locations where access to traditional power sources may be limited.
The design and development of an energy-efficient solar-powered portable oil extractor entail integrating renewable energy sources and innovative technology to create a sustainable and portable solution for oil extraction. By harnessing solar energy through photovoltaic panels, the extractor operates without reliance on traditional power sources, reducing both operational costs and environmental impact.
The innovation of the solar-powered portable oil extractor efficiently extracts oil from various seeds with minimal environmental impact. Key components of the extractor include efficient motors and pumps optimized for low power consumption, as well as lightweight and durable materials for portability and ease of transportation. Advanced filtration and extraction mechanisms ensure high-quality oil production while minimizing energy use.
This compact device is user-friendly and produces clean oil suitable for food and medicinal purposes. Our implementation has achieved a 39.8% increase in efficiency using a 24V DC motor, successfully extracting 196 mL of mustard oil from 500 g of seeds in just 4.2 minutes. This performance is significantly better than traditional induction motors, which are slower and less efficient. Additionally, smart controls and monitoring systems enable remote operation and real-time performance tracking, enhancing efficiency and user convenience. By leveraging cutting-edge engineering principles and renewable energy technologies, this solution addresses the growing demand for sustainable and portable oil extraction systems in remote or off-grid locations, empowering communities with access to essential resources while promoting environmental stewardship.

This project is fully funded by Energy Saving Trust, UK
